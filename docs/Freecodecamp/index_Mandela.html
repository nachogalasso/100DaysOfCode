Nelson Mandela

Madiba, "The Father of the Nation"

Let´s get to know a little about his life

  • 1918 - Mandela was born on July 18 in Umtata (in Mvezo village)
  • 1933 - He went to Clarkebury Methodist High School (in Engcobo) to completo his secondary education
  • 1937 - Moved to Healdtown, the Methodist college in Fort Beaufort
  • 1939 - Mandela began work on a BA degree at the University of Fort Hare. He studied English, anthropology, politics, "native administration", and Roman Dutch law.
  • 1940 - His father arranges a marriage and Mandela decides to run away to Johannesburg. To continue his higher education, Mandela signed up to a University of South Africa correspondence course, working on his bachelor's degree at night.
  • 1944 - Mandela met Evelyn Mase and got married with her in October. With her he had a son and a daughter, but her daughter died of meningitis
  • 1947 - Mandela was elected to the executive committee of the ANC's Transvaal Province branch
  • 1950 - Mandela took Xuma's place on the ANC national executive in March and that same year was elected national president of the ANCYL
  • 1952 - He was arrested under the Suppression of Communism Act and stood trial as one of the 21 accused. Found guilty of "statutory communism" but their sentence of nine months hard labour was suspended for two years.
  • 1953 - In August, Mandela open a law firm with his friend Tambo in Johannesburg. It was the only African-run law firm in the country
  • 1956 - In December, Mandela was arrested alongside most of the ANC national executive, and accused of "high treason" against the state. In October 1958 the charges were dismissed, but in November they were accused with high treason by advocating violent revolution.
  • 1961 - Six years after the Treason Trial began, the judges produced a verdict of not guilty, ruling that there was insufficient evidence to convict the accused of "high treason", since they had advocated neither communism nor violent revolution; the outcome embarrassed the government. Mandela, Sisulu, and Slovo co-founded Umkhonto we Sizwe
  • 1962 - On August 5th, police captured Mandela. He was jailed in Johannesburg's Marshall Square prison, Mandela was charged with inciting workers' strikes and leaving the country without permission
  • 1964 - On June 12th, justice De Wet found Mandela and two of his co-accused guilty on all four charges; although the prosecution had called for the death sentence to be applied, the judge instead condemned them to life imprisonment. They were transferred from Pretoria to the prison on Robben Island, remaining there for the next 18 years
  • 1982 - In April, Mandela was transferred to Pollsmoor Prison in Tokai, Cape Town. Conditions at Pollsmoor were better than at Robben Island.
  • 1988 - December, Mandela was moved to Victor Verster Prison near Paarl
  • 1990 - On February 1990, president De Klerk and announcing Mandela's unconditional release. Leaving Victor Verster Prison on 11 February, Mandela held Winnie's hand in front of amassed crowds and the press; the event was broadcast live across the world. Mandela proceeded on an African tour.
  • 1991 - A national peace conference was held in Johannesburg at which Mandela, Buthelezi and de Klerk signed a peace accord, though the violence continued.
  • 1994 - The newly elected National Assembly's first act was to formally elect Mandela as South Africa's first black chief executive. Presiding over the transition from apartheid minority rule to a multicultural democracy, Mandela saw national reconciliation as the primary task of his presidency.
  • 1999 - He retires from politics to continue activism and philanthropy working with the Nelson Mandela Foundation
  • 2013 - After suffering from a prolonged respiratory infection, Mandela died on 5 December at the age of 95. Zuma publicly announced his death on television, proclaiming ten days of national mourning, a memorial service held at Johannesburg's FNB Stadium
